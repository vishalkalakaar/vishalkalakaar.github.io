How I got here

From school benches to building business software

A year-by-year look at school, college, the detour through design school, and the three jobs that turned into a software development career — plus what people who've worked with me have said along the way.

My journey

2012 — 2026

2012

High School — PDS Senior Secondary School

Completed High School (10th) from PDS Senior Secondary School.

2014

Intermediate — PDS Senior Secondary School

Completed Intermediate (12th) from the same school, before moving on to a science degree.

2014 — 2017

B.Sc (PCM)

Studied Physics, Chemistry and Mathematics at the graduation level — a science foundation rather than a computer science one.

2017 — 2018

A year to figure out the next step

Took a year after graduation before deciding to pursue design and web development instead of continuing in pure science.

2018 — 2020

Graphic, Web Design & Development — Arena Animation

Trained in graphic design, web design and front-end development fundamentals — the course that pointed directly at a development career.

2020 — 2021

First job — Laravel Developer at Amiss Engineering

Started my first development job building and maintaining web applications with Laravel, PHP and MySQL.

2021 — 2022

Website Designer at Joon Corporation (JoonWeb)

Moved to JoonWeb, a website-builder product, designing and building responsive templates and front-end components.

2022 — Present

DotNet Developer at RR Finance

Joined RR Finance as a DotNet Developer, building business applications with ASP.NET, ASP.NET MVC, C#, SQL Server and Web API — still here today.

2023

Started MCA — GLA University, Mathura

Began an MCA through distance learning at GLA University, Mathura, to formalize the computer science side of a self-taught development career.

2025

Started professional portrait commissions

Took pencil portraits from a personal hobby to paid commissions — gifts, family portraits and custom orders for clients found through Instagram.

2026

Launched this portfolio

Brought both sides of the work — development and art — together into one portfolio site, with a dedicated gallery for sketches under @vishal_kalakaar.
